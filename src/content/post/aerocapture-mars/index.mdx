A group design project from my Aerospace course at Imperial College London, investigating how aerocapture could slash the fuel budget for a Mars orbiting mission.

I was responsible for the interplanetary trajectory design. Unlike typical missions where the flight path angle can be corrected with fuel before atmospheric entry, an aerocapture mission lives or dies by the accuracy of the insertion angle. So I flipped the traditional patched conics approach; starting from the required insertion angle and working backwards. More in the report below!
