Cool group design project in my Aerospace course at Imperial College London. The objective was to investigate how using aerocapture could reduce the fuel needed for an orbiting mission to Mars. 

I was responsible for the interplanetary trajectory design. Unlike most missions, where the FPA can be corrected with fuel prior to entry in the atmosphere, the success of the mission heavily depends on the accuracy of the insertion angle into the atmosphere. Because of this, I applied the traditional patched conics approach a bit differently, first defining the angle of insertion and working backwards from there. More information can be found on the report below!
