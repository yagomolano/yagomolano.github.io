In this project, which I was a cofounder, we looked at designing a Hyperloop vehicle to participate in international competitions!

From the leading perspective, I ran the marketing campaign to recruit students, having over 100 joining students even though it was our first year running!

From the technical side, I was leading 'three' technical teams bundled into one - levitation, suspension and breaking.

I personally developed a "lightweight" model for a rotational hallback array, to give a rough estimate of power -> spinning velocity -> magnetic field strength -> track repulsion force. This was then integrated into a SIMULINK model to estimate the dynamics of the pod, and choose stiffness of the suspension to ensure deviations from target position remain within the set limits and vibrations are not too uncomfortable.

We also investigated how we could modulate the speed of rotation for active control.

You could say this started my passion for control! :) (you could also argue that skipping learning with car suspension and jumping straight into a problem of such complicated dynamics was not the best idea...)
