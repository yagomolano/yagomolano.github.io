As a co-founder of this project, we set out to design a Hyperloop vehicle for international competitions!

On the leadership side, I ran the recruitment campaign (including the video above) — bringing in over 100 students in our very first year!

On the technical side, I led three teams bundled into one: levitation, suspension and braking.

My technical highlight was developing a lightweight analytical model for a rotational Halbach array in Wolfram Mathematica — estimating the chain from power → spinning velocity → magnetic field strength → track repulsion force. This fed into a Simulink model to simulate pod dynamics and tune the suspension stiffness, keeping position deviations within limits and vibrations at a comfortable level.

We also investigated how we could modulate the speed of rotation for active control. 

You could say this is where my passion for control started :) (you could also argue that skipping car suspension modelling and jumping straight into Hyperloop dynamics was not the wisest first move...)
