One of the coolest projects I've worked on! 

This master's thesis tackled robust optimal control for lunar landers, specifically, how to achieve pinpoint landing despite real-world uncertainties. I proposed and investigated a novel local reduction approach, demonstrating its applicability to soft landing scenarios and benchmarking accuracy and computational efficiency against state-of-the-art methods.

The work covers the development of a nonlinear, non-convex trajectory optimisation model, including convergence analysis and validation. It characterises the key uncertainties in lunar landing and their best modelling strategies, then examines the local reduction algorithm's convergence, scenario handling and speed. Open-loop and closed-loop trajectory propagation are evaluated through Monte Carlo simulations.

**Key results:** the method reduces the landing ellipse by over an order of magnitude in open loop and cuts the standard deviation by two orders of magnitude. It accurately predicts worst-case fuel requirements and provides near-real-time disturbance predictions for each control input — opening up real-time validation possibilities. In closed loop, centimetre-level pinpoint landing accuracy is achievable with a decreasing-horizon implementation.

Beyond lunar landing, the thesis explores applications including neural network training, real-time V&V, and robust trajectory planning for unforeseen scenarios.
