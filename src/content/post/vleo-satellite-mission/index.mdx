This project looked at the feasibility of a mission to fly in very low earth orbit (VLEO) focused only on TRL9 (flight ready) technology, for a synthetic aperture radar (SAR) mission. Something that I found during this project was that there are many requirements when combining SAR and VLEO in terms of optimum shape and size, making the necessary trade-offs much harder to wrap your head around when compared to traditional satellite design. Because of this, I devised a model-based optimisation approach that looked to model all subcomponents, including overall shape based on drag, so that the "optimum" minimum mass design could be found. It gave some pretty interesting findings, and led to [ST Engineering Satellite Systems](https://www.stengg.com/en/satellite-systems/) moving on with the project.
